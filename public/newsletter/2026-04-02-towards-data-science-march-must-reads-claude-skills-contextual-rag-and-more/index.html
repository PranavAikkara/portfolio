March Must-Reads: Claude Skills, Contextual RAG, and More

Three months into 2026, it's becoming very clear that AI-assisted coding is the dominant theme of the year (so far, at least). 

 

From Claude Code skill-building to incorporating coding agents into existing workflows, data and ML practitioners are at the forefront of a rapid transformation. It comes with massive potential for efficiency and growth, but also for disruption and underwhelming results.

 

Our most-read articles of the past month reflect that excitement — and explore that tension. They also cover a broad range of other topics for those of you suffering from vibe coding fatigue. Let's dig in. 

Claude Skills and Subagents: Escaping the Prompt Engineering Hamster Wheel

Context bloat has become a real issue for product builders trying to automate and streamline repetiti elements within their work. Ruben Broekx shows how we can tackle this problem by leveraging the power of skills and subagents. He explains how they work, how they differ from MCP, and how all these disparate tools can come together into a cohesive workflow.
